How It Works

At 03:05 UTC each day, the system checks whether BTC is trading above its 20-period EMA on the daily chart. If below, no trade is placed (blacklist day).

If the trend filter passes, the system sells 1 put option at the strike closest to -10 delta (~90% probability of expiring OTM).

The option expires at 08:00 UTC the following day, giving a hold window of approximately 29 hours.

The position is actively managed: a 90% take-profit locks in gains early, while a 70% stop-loss caps losses before the option moves deep in-the-money.

If neither TP nor SL is triggered, the option expires worthless and the full premium is retained as profit.

Strategy Edge & Risk Analysis

Why This Works

  • Theta Decay Harvesting — 1-DTE options have the steepest time decay. ~29-hour hold captures maximum theta per unit of time.
  • OTM Probability Advantage — At -10 delta, puts have ~90% probability of expiring OTM. The structural base rate is high.
  • EMA 20 Trend Filter — Avoids selling puts into downtrends, the single most dangerous scenario for short puts.
  • Asymmetric TP/SL — 90% TP locks gains early; 70% SL caps losses before options go deep ITM.
  • Profit Factor 1.97 — For every $1 lost, the strategy earns $1.97. Above 1.5 is considered strong.

Risk Factors

  • Tail Risk — Extreme BTC flash crashes could gap through the stop-loss before it triggers, especially during illiquid hours.
  • Volatility Regime — Sustained high-IV means larger premiums but also larger dollar losses per stopped trade.
  • Liquidity — 1-DTE BTC options may have wider spreads; the backtest may not fully capture real-world slippage.
  • Filter Dependency — The strategy's risk management relies on the EMA filter. A regime change that the EMA misses could lead to deeper drawdowns.
